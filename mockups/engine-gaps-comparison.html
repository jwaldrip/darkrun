the recommendation, honestly

There are only three things I'd actually build: D1 (quality-gate execution) first — it's the one real correctness gap left; then the content-model batch (E2–E7); then the run-level review wiring (C4/C5). After that the ledger is, in my honest read, done enough.

What I'd argue against building (the skip rows): C1 multi-signal elaborate, B5 verifier nonce, B7 dispatch leases, B9 fix-chain worktrees, D4 coverage ack, F2 human_write, F4 review_stamp, F5 presence grace, G3 clarifications, G5 persisted sessions. Each solves a problem darkrun either doesn't have or solves another way — porting them just because the predecessor had them would be cargo-culting. Build any only when its triggering condition actually shows up (named in each row).

Deliberate design differences (the keep rows) aren't work at all — the fixed six stations, revert-self-heal over auto-restamp, hooks over an FSM checksum. Flip one only to change the design. A few small defer items (plugin_version stamp, external_refs, draft-PR status, proof upload) are worth doing whenever their integration lands — not urgent.
