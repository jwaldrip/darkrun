where it landed, honestly

Every confident gap is closed. 27 of 44 mechanisms are built and committed — including the ones I had initially wanted to skip and was corrected on: C1 collaboration backpressure, B9 per-unit + per-fix worktree isolation, F4 parallel review stamp, D4/E5 cross-station input coverage (template and runtime). The last one, E5, started as "structured inputs" and was reframed: the {stage,output} typing was genuinely redundant with D4, but the runtime per-unit coverage check it implied was missing — and lands earlier than a reviewer would.

Intentionally not built. The keep rows are deliberate design differences, not work — the fixed six stations, revert-self-heal over auto-restamp, hooks over an FSM checksum, the discrete PR-merge poll. The skip rows are redundant in darkrun's model (dispatch leases backstopped by the deadlock halt; run_quality_gates subsumed by D1; applies_to by per-station rosters; clarifications by the run doc; in-memory sessions by design).

Deferred (the defer rows) — real, but waiting on a trigger or integration: B5 verifier nonce, C5 run-level mode shaping, D5 proof-upload, F2 human_write, F5 presence grace, G1 plugin_version, G2 external_refs, G4 draft-PR status. None block engine correctness. Full per-line detail in the companion engine-parity-gaps.md and the row-level engine-parity-gaps.csv.
